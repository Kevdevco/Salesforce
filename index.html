This document presents findings from primary research conducted by Group 13B as part of the International Digital Marketing Week 2026 (TU Dublin √ó Salesforce). Our research question: Where does AI deliver the most value in B2B sales today, and where is its potential underused?

Using a mixed-methods approach ‚Äî structured surveys combined with open-text qualitative responses ‚Äî we gathered insights from 17 B2B professionals across Dublin, spanning Sales/BizDev, Management, Operations, and Marketing roles.

Top value area: Administrative automation (53%) is currently the biggest demonstrated AI value area; Lead generation (41%) and Customer insights (35%) follow.
Top barriers: Skills gap (59%), Cost (47%), and Data Quality (41%) are the primary blockers preventing wider AI adoption.
Key gap: Strategic AI uses ‚Äî retention, forecasting, relationship intelligence ‚Äî are flagged as significantly underused despite clear demand.

Research Approach & Instruments

Approach: Mixed-methods (structured survey + open-text responses / brief intercept notes). Methods were chosen to balance speed and depth for fieldwork conducted across Dublin on a single day.

Sample & Recruitment: 17 completed structured surveys from B2B practitioners. Recruitment via in-person intercepts (Dublin city fieldwork) and a digital survey distributed to local professional networks. Sampling purposefully targeted practitioners with sales/CRM responsibilities.

Role Breakdown: Sales/BizDev (7), Management (6), Operations (3), Marketing (1). Organisation sizes: SME 1‚Äì49 staff (10), Medium 50+ (4), Start-ups (3).

Survey Instrument: 12 questions covering a mix of multi-select, Likert scale, ranking, and open-text formats. Topics included: current tools in use, where AI adds value, underused areas, barriers to adoption, AI maturity/usage levels, confidence, and future outlook.

‚ö†Ô∏è Limitations: Small sample (n=17) ‚Äî this research is exploratory, not conclusive. Results reflect Dublin-based B2B practitioners and may not generalise beyond that context. Mixed-methods fieldwork prioritised speed and representativeness within the time-limited brief.

Current State of AI in Dublin B2B Sales

What tools are in use and how deeply is AI embedded?

CRM adoption is strong at 76%, driven largely by Salesforce and HubSpot. However, only 35% report using explicit AI tools, and extensive AI adoption remains low ‚Äî just 2 out of 17 respondents. This suggests significant room for growth, particularly in bridging the gap between CRM usage and AI-powered CRM features.

Where AI Adds Value Today

The areas where respondents see AI delivering the most impact right now

Administrative task automation leads at 53%, confirming that AI's most immediate impact is reducing manual, repetitive work ‚Äî data entry, scheduling, report generation. Lead generation (41%) and customer insights (35%) follow, both suggesting AI is beginning to contribute to revenue-facing activities. However, forecasting sits at just 18%, indicating that predictive and strategic AI capabilities remain underexploited.

Untapped Potential & Barriers

Where AI is underused and what's holding adoption back

While AI is proving its value in admin automation, respondents identified significant untapped potential in higher-value areas. Customer retention leads the "underused" list at 53%, followed by relationship management and forecasting (both 41%). Meanwhile, the skills gap (59%) outranks even cost (47%) as the top barrier ‚Äî suggesting that training, not pricing, is the most effective lever for adoption.

5 Key Strategic Insights

Headline findings and implications for Salesforce

1

AI Is Winning the Admin War, But Losing the Strategy Battle

Evidence: 53% cite admin tasks as top value area, while Customer Retention (53%) and Strategic Decision-Making (35%) are the most "underused" areas.
Salesforce implication: Position Agentforce as a strategic partner, not just an admin assistant. Highlight retention & planning use cases.
2

The Skills Gap Is the #1 Barrier ‚Äî Bigger Than Cost

Evidence: 60% cite lack of skills as a barrier, even among active users.
Salesforce implication: Invest heavily in SME-specific onboarding paths. "Training is the unlock."
3

Data Quality Is the Silent Killer of Trust

Evidence: "Garbage in, garbage out" was the dominant open-text theme.
Salesforce implication: Bundle Data Cloud features as essential prerequisites for AI success.
4

Confidence Follows Usage

Evidence: 100% of heavy users are confident. Non-users are not.
Focus on "Quick Wins" to build early confidence.
5

SMEs Want AI Scaled to Them

Evidence: 67% of SMEs see AI as essential but feel tools are "too big."
Create lightweight "Essentials" AI packages.

Evidence-Based Recommendations for Salesforce

Five actionable recommendations ‚Äî each tied to our primary research evidence

These recommendations translate our research findings into operational actions for Salesforce. Each includes a specific initiative, its evidence base, and expected impact on B2B sales adoption.

1

Create SME "Essentials" AI Bundles

Pre-configured, low-cost AI packages focused on admin automation + lead gen for SMEs. Simple installation with 1-click onboarding to reduce time-to-value.

Impact: Rapid adoption in SMEs, increased CRM usage and trust. Addresses insight that 67% of SMEs see AI as essential but find current tools too complex.
2

Offer Role-Specific Micro-Training

Short (20‚Äì60 min) interactive modules for Sales, Ops, and Management that include sample data, before/after workflows, and editable templates. Include "trust calibration" exercises showing how to validate AI outputs.

Impact: Lowers the #1 barrier (skills gap at 59%), improves adoption, and builds the confidence that drives continued usage.
3

Bundle a Data Health Starter

A free or low-cost audit tool integrated into Salesforce that scans core CRM records and returns a concise "data health" score plus a prioritised fix list with guided cleanup workflows.

Impact: Tackles the "garbage in, garbage out" problem; increases readiness for strategic AI features. Addresses data quality barrier (41%).
4

Pilot a "Strategic Account Planner" AI

A pilot product surfacing account-level strategic insights ‚Äî churn risk, cross-sell opportunities, relationship mapping ‚Äî built on cleaned CRM + public signal enrichment. Initially delivered as an advisory pilot with a dedicated CSM.

Impact: Demonstrates strategic value beyond admin; shows internal sales leaders ROI for larger purchases. Addresses the strategy gap.
5

Measure and Publicise "Quick Win" KPIs

For each deployment, report 3 measurable KPIs: time saved on admin tasks, increase in qualified leads, and improvement in forecasting accuracy. Publish anonymised case studies for SMEs and mid-market.

Impact: Builds social proof and reduces perceived risk for new customers. 100% of heavy AI users are confident ‚Äî proving quick wins converts the sceptics.
